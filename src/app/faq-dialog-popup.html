FAQ

Where is this data collected from?

Data is collected through the Dataverse Metrics API. For more information, visit: guides.dataverse.org/en/latest/api/metrics.html

Who created this tool?

The Dataverse Team at the University of Toronto Libraries.

What is Borealis (All)?

Borealis (All) refers to the entirety of Borealis — meaning the data represents all dataverses combined.

Why isn’t this specific dataverse included?

It may take time for a dataverse to be reflected in the source data. Also, dataverses must be published to be included.

Why isn’t my dataverse displayed in French?

Dataverses are displayed in their original, published language.

What is Legacy?

Legacy is a structured data export that was previously hosted on the older version of the Dataverse Metrics Page.

What is DataCite / MakeDataCount?

MakeDataCount is a third-party, community-led project that tracks metrics such as downloads, views, and citations for files, datasets, and dataverses within Borealis.

It’s the first of the month — why isn’t this month included?

It can take a few business days (typically 3–5) for the current month’s data to appear in the metrics.

Who can I contact for other questions, improvements, discovered bugs and feature requests?

Please reach out to the Dataverse Team at the University of Toronto Libraries.
